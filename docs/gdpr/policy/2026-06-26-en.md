# Privacy Policy

This page explains how Ingmarsöloppet collects and processes your personal data. We've tried to write it as plainly as possible — no boilerplate, just what we actually do.

## 1. Who is the data controller?

Ingmarsöloppet (here "we", "us") is the data controller for the processing described below. Contact: **kontakt@ingmarsoloppet.se**.

The Swedish supervisory authority is the **Authority for Privacy Protection (IMY)**, [imy.se](https://imy.se).

## 2. What data do we collect?

When you register for a race we record:

- **Name** — for the registration, start list and results.
- **Date of birth** — for age class and the under-13 guardian-consent threshold.
- **Gender** — for age class and statistics.
- **Email** — as the identity for your account and to send race information.
- **IP address** — briefly at registration, for spam and bot prevention.

On race day we add **result data**: bib number, finish time, any splits, and any notes about conditions or disqualification.

For **participants under 13** we also collect a guardian's email and an active consent from the guardian (via clicking a magic link).

In your browser we only store:

- Your chosen language and theme (in `localStorage`).
- A login cookie while you are signed into `/my-data` or the admin panel.

## 3. What do we use the data for?

| Purpose | What it means | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Run the race registration | Start list, age class, race communication, results | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) |
| Public results on the web | Show name, age class and time in result lists | Legitimate interest — you can opt out (Art. 6(1)(f)) |
| Newsletter | Information about upcoming races and similar, if you opt in | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) |
| Bot and spam prevention | Short-lived IP counting at the registration form | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) |
| Security logs | Activity log for admins and DSR events | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) |

## 4. Public results

Result lists for Ingmarsöloppet show your **full name, age class and finish time** by default. This is how Swedish running tradition works, but it is still **voluntary**: you can turn off the display at any time via `/my-data`. Your name is then replaced by "Anonym" in all public lists, while bib number, age class and time remain so the result list is complete.

For **participants under 13**, the full name is never shown publicly, regardless of consent. Only first name plus initial is used.

## 5. Who else do we share data with?

We use the following sub-processors to run the service:

- **Amazon Web Services EMEA SARL** — compute, storage and email. All data is stored in region **eu-north-1 (Stockholm)**. Static pages are distributed via CloudFront edge within Europe.

We don't share data with anyone beyond that. No ad networks, no analytics, no social-media plugins.

## 6. How long do we keep your data?

| What | How long |
|---|---|
| Registration without start (DNS/no-show) | Removed 6 months after the race |
| Completed race + public-results consent | Kept indefinitely (your race history) |
| Completed race without public-results consent | Anonymised 36 months after your most recent race. The clock resets every time you participate in a new race |
| Account with no remaining runners | 30 days (so you can change your mind) |
| Admin activity log | 24 months |
| Security logs (CloudWatch) | 30 days |
| Backups (DynamoDB PITR) | 35 days |

## 7. Your rights

You have the right to:

- **See** all the data we have about you — directly in `/my-data` (sign in via a magic link to your email).
- **Correct** mistakes — directly in `/my-data` or by emailing us.
- **Erase** your account and all linked runners — directly in `/my-data`.
- **Restrict** the processing — contact us.
- **Receive** your data in a machine-readable format (JSON) — directly in `/my-data`.
- **Object** to public results or the newsletter — directly in `/my-data`.

You also have the right to **complain to IMY** ([imy.se](https://imy.se)) if you believe we are not handling your data correctly.

We normally respond within 5 working days and complete the request within 30 days (up to 90 days in complex cases).

## 8. Cookies and browser storage

We do **not** use tracking cookies, analytics cookies or marketing cookies.

**Session cookies (set only after you sign in):**

- `the-run-session` — admin session, 8 hours.
- `the-run-dsr-session` — `/my-data` session after clicking your magic link, 2 hours.
- `the-run-auth-challenge` — short-lived bridge between password and two-factor code during admin login, 5 minutes.

All three are HttpOnly, Secure, and SameSite=Strict. They disappear when you sign out, the session expires, or you close the browser.

**`localStorage` (set only when you actively click the relevant switcher):**

- `the-run.locale` — your chosen language (sv/en).
- `the-run.theme` — your chosen theme (light/dark/system).

Because we don't use tracking and every session cookie is strictly necessary for the service you requested, you don't need to click away any "cookie banner" here. The full analysis is in `docs/gdpr/cookie-analysis.md` in our source code.

## 9. Security

- All data is encrypted at rest (AWS-managed key) and in transit (HTTPS).
- Backups for 35 days (DynamoDB Point-in-Time Recovery).
- Admin accounts require two-factor authentication (TOTP).
- Passwords are stored with bcrypt (cost 12).
- Login attempts are limited with automatic lockout after repeated failures.

## 10. Changes to the policy

We version each update with a version date. Minor wording adjustments bump a *revision* within the same version; larger changes are published as a brand new version.

In `/my-data` you can always see exactly which version and revision you accepted when you registered — and read that specific text even if we have updated the policy since.
